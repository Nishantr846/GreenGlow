Common Diseases

Powdery Mildew

Cause: Fungal spores thriving in dry, warm environments.

Symptoms: White, powdery spots on leaves, stems, and buds.

Cure:

  • Improve air circulation.
  • Spray neem oil or sulfur-based fungicide.
  • Remove affected leaves.

Root Rot

Cause: Overwatering and poorly drained soil.

Symptoms: Yellowing leaves, mushy and blackened roots.

Cure:

  • Remove affected roots.
  • Repot with fresh, well-draining soil.
  • Reduce watering frequency.

Leaf Spot

Cause: Fungi (like Cercospora) or bacteria.

Symptoms: Brown, black, or yellow spots on leaves, sometimes with a yellow halo.

Cure:

  • Remove infected leaves.
  • Apply copper-based fungicides.
  • Water the base of the plant, not the leaves.

Rust

Cause: Fungal infection under humid conditions.

Symptoms: Orange, yellow, or brown pustules under leaves.

Cure:

  • Remove and destroy infected leaves.
  • Use sulfur or copper fungicides.
  • Provide better air circulation.

Botrytis (Gray Mold)

Cause: Fungus thriving on dead/dying tissues.

Symptoms: Gray fuzzy mold on flowers, leaves, and stems.

Cure:

  • Remove affected parts immediately.
  • Reduce humidity around plants.
  • Apply fungicide treatments.

Downy Mildew

Cause: Water molds attacking under humid, cool conditions.

Symptoms: Yellow patches on upper leaf surfaces, gray fuzz underneath.

Cure:

  • Improve ventilation and drainage.
  • Use phosphonate fungicides.
  • Remove and destroy affected leaves.

Anthracnose

Cause: Fungal pathogens (e.g., Colletotrichum).

Symptoms: Dark, sunken lesions on leaves, stems, fruits.

Cure:

  • Prune infected parts.
  • Apply systemic fungicides.
  • Avoid overhead watering.

Fusarium Wilt

Cause: Soil-borne fungus blocking water transport.

Symptoms: Yellowing, wilting, and eventual death of plant.

Cure:

  • Use disease-resistant plant varieties.
  • Improve soil drainage.
  • Remove and destroy affected plants.

Bacterial Blight

Cause: Water-splashed bacteria entering wounds.

Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions that turn brown and dry out.

Cure:

  • Remove affected plant parts.
  • Apply bactericides containing copper.
  • Avoid overhead irrigation.

Mosaic Virus

Cause: Viral infection often spread by insects (like aphids).

Symptoms: Mottled patterns of light and dark green on leaves, stunted growth.

Cure:

  • Remove and destroy infected plants.
  • Control insect vectors.
  • Use virus-free seeds and plants.

Edema

Cause: Overabsorption of water causing cell rupture.

Symptoms: Blisters, corky growths, or wart-like bumps on leaves.

Cure:

  • Improve air circulation and light.
  • Water plants less frequently.
  • Ensure proper drainage.

Black Spot

Cause: Fungal infection (Diplocarpon rosae) in humid environments.

Symptoms: Black, round spots with yellow halos on leaves.

Cure:

  • Remove affected leaves.
  • Use fungicides like chlorothalonil.
  • Keep foliage dry and clean.

Scorching

Cause: Excessive heat, drought, salt, or wind damage.

Symptoms: Browning of leaf edges and tips.

Cure:

  • Provide sufficient water (especially during dry periods).
  • Use mulch to retain soil moisture.
  • Offer partial shade during peak heat.

Crown Rot

Cause: Fungal attack at the plant base, mostly by Phytophthora.

Symptoms: Wilting, yellowing, and plant collapse at soil level.

Cure:

  • Improve soil drainage immediately.
  • Avoid watering the crown area directly.
  • Use fungicidal drenches.

Verticillium Wilt

Cause: Soil-borne fungus invading roots and blocking water transport.

Symptoms: Yellowing between veins, wilting, and stunted growth.

Cure:

  • Remove infected plants.
  • Use resistant plant varieties.
  • Solarize soil to kill pathogens.

Sooty Mold

Cause: Fungal growth on honeydew secreted by insects like aphids or scale.

Symptoms: Black, soot-like coating on leaves and stems.

Cure:

  • Control insect pests first (aphids, whiteflies).
  • Wash off the mold with water and mild soap.
  • Use horticultural oils if severe.

Blight

Cause: Fungi or bacteria under wet conditions.

Symptoms: Rapid browning and death of leaves, flowers, and shoots.

Cure:

  • Prune and destroy infected parts immediately.
  • Apply fungicides early if weather is wet.
  • Improve spacing for better air flow.

Clubroot

Cause: Soil-borne pathogen (Plasmodiophora brassicae).

Symptoms: Swollen, distorted roots; wilting during heat; stunted growth.

Cure:

  • Raise soil pH with lime (make it less acidic).
  • Rotate crops and avoid planting susceptible crops repeatedly.
  • Use resistant plant varieties.
