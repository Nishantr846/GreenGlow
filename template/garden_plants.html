Floral Plants

Rose (Rosa)

Roses are one of the most popular garden flowers known for their vibrant colors and lovely fragrance. They come in various varieties, including hybrid tea roses, climbing roses, and floribundas.

Care Tips:

  • Requires well-drained soil.
  • Needs plenty of sunlight (at least 6 hours a day).
  • Prune regularly to remove dead or damaged branches.

Tulip (Tulipa)

Tulips are spring-blooming flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, yellow, and purple. They are known for their elegant shape and vibrant petals.

Care Tips:

  • Plant bulbs in fall, about 6 to 8 inches deep.
  • Requires full sun for optimal growth.
  • Water when the soil feels dry.

Sunflower (Helianthus annuus)

Known for their large, yellow blooms, sunflowers are tall plants that follow the sun throughout the day. They make excellent cut flowers and attract pollinators.

Care Tips:

  • Plant seeds in well-drained, fertile soil.
  • Needs full sunlight (at least 6 hours of direct sunlight).
  • Water deeply but infrequently.

Marigold (Tagetes)

Marigolds are easy-to-grow flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. They are known for their pest-repelling properties and long blooming period.

Care Tips:

  • Thrives in full sunlight.
  • Water regularly but ensure good drainage.
  • Remove dead flowers to encourage new blooms.

Daisy (Bellis perennis)

Daisies are simple yet elegant flowers known for their white petals and yellow centers. They are popular in gardens due to their cheerful appearance and easy care. Daisies can thrive in various garden settings and are often used for borders or as ground cover.

Care Tips:

  • Prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade.
  • Well-drained, moderately fertile soil is ideal.
  • Water regularly, but allow the soil to dry out between waterings.

Lavender (Lavandula)

Lavender is a fragrant perennial flower that produces purple, blue, or pink blooms. It is perfect for creating a calming atmosphere in your garden and attracts bees and butterflies.

Care Tips:

  • Requires full sun.
  • Well-drained soil is essential.
  • Prune after flowering to maintain shape and encourage new growth.

Daffodil (Narcissus)

Daffodils are cheerful spring flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, white, and orange. They are known for their trumpet-like shape and are often seen as a symbol of renewal.

Care Tips:

  • Plant bulbs in the fall.
  • Requires well-drained, slightly acidic soil.
  • Water after planting and during the growing season.

Lily (Lilium)

Lilies are large, fragrant flowers that come in many varieties, including oriental, trumpet, and Asiatic lilies. They are often used in bouquets and have an elegant look.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in a sunny location with well-drained soil.
  • Water regularly, but ensure the soil is not soggy.
  • Deadhead spent blooms to encourage new growth.

Peony (Paeonia)

Peonies are large, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. They are known for their lush, fragrant petals.

Care Tips:

  • Requires well-drained, fertile soil.
  • Full sunlight is ideal, but partial shade is acceptable in hotter climates.
  • Keep the soil consistently moist, especially during dry periods.

Zinnia (Zinnia elegans)

Zinnias are bright, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, orange, and yellow. They are heat-tolerant and thrive in full sun.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in well-drained, loamy soil.
  • Needs full sun and moderate watering.
  • Remove dead flowers regularly to encourage new blooms.

Begonia (Begonia semperflorens)

Begonias are known for their colorful blooms and ornamental foliage. They come in shades of red, pink, and white, and are perfect for shaded or partially shaded areas in the garden.

Care Tips:

  • Prefers partial shade or full shade.
  • Keep the soil moist but not soggy.
  • Regularly remove dead leaves and flowers to promote new growth.

Geranium (Pelargonium)

Geraniums are known for their vibrant and colorful blooms that come in shades of red, pink, purple, and white. They are perfect for containers or hanging baskets.

Care Tips:

  • Requires full sun.
  • Water regularly, but ensure the soil is well-drained.
  • Deadhead flowers to promote continuous blooming.

Hydrangea (Hydrangea macrophylla)

Hydrangeas are large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors including blue, pink, and white. They are perfect for adding a romantic touch to the garden.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in partial shade to full sun.
  • Requires well-drained soil that is rich in organic matter.
  • Water regularly, especially during dry spells.

Clematis (Clematis)

Clematis are climbing plants that produce large, stunning flowers in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, red, and white. They are perfect for trellises or fences.

Care Tips:

  • Requires full sun but prefers the roots to be shaded.
  • Provide a trellis or structure to support the vines.
  • Prune after flowering to maintain shape.

Cosmos (Cosmos bipinnatus)

Cosmos are tall, elegant flowers that bloom in a variety of colors including pink, white, red, and purple. They are known for attracting butterflies.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in full sun and well-drained soil.
  • Water when the soil feels dry.
  • Deadhead regularly to encourage more blooms.

Chrysanthemum (Chrysanthemum morifolium)

Chrysanthemums, often called mums, come in a variety of colors and bloom in late summer to fall. They are known for their ruffled petals and are commonly used in autumn gardens.

Care Tips:

  • Needs full sun for optimal blooming.
  • Water consistently, but avoid overwatering.
  • Prune after blooming to encourage new growth.

Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea)

Foxgloves are tall, spiky flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, white, and yellow. They are excellent for adding vertical interest to your garden.

Care Tips:

  • Requires full sun to partial shade.
  • Needs well-drained, slightly acidic soil.
  • Keep soil consistently moist during the growing season.

Aster (Aster amellus)

Asters are late-blooming flowers that come in shades of purple, pink, and white. They add color to the garden in the fall, making them great for extending the growing season.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in full sun or partial shade.
  • Requires well-drained soil.
  • Water regularly, especially during dry spells.

Salvia (Salvia officinalis)

Salvias are perennial plants with spikes of colorful flowers in shades of purple, red, and blue. They are popular for their aromatic leaves and ability to attract pollinators.

Care Tips:

  • Needs full sun and well-drained soil.
  • Water deeply, but allow the soil to dry between watering.
  • Prune to maintain shape and promote new growth.

Pansy (Viola tricolor var. hortensis)

Pansies are colorful, cool-season flowers that come in various shades including purple, yellow, orange, and blue. They have large, delicate petals with a distinctive face-like appearance.

Care Tips:

  • Prefers cool weather and partial sun.
  • Water consistently, but avoid waterlogging.
  • Deadhead regularly to promote continuous blooms.

Sweet Pea (Lathyrus odoratus)

Sweet peas are fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, such as pink, purple, and white. They are climbing plants, perfect for trellises, and their sweet fragrance is a garden favorite.

Care Tips:

  • Needs full sun and well-drained soil.
  • Water regularly but ensure the soil is not soggy.
  • Remove spent blooms to encourage more flowers.

Dahlia (Dahlia pinnata)

Dahlias are stunning flowers that come in a wide range of colors and forms, including pom-pom, cactus, and dinner-plate varieties. They are excellent for adding color to the garden.

Care Tips:

  • Needs full sun and well-drained soil.
  • Water regularly but ensure good drainage.
  • Protect tubers in winter if growing in cooler climates.

Impatiens (Impatiens walleriana)

Impatiens are shade-loving flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, purple, and white. They are perfect for adding color to shady garden spots.

Care Tips:

  • Prefers partial to full shade.
  • Keep soil consistently moist.
  • Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

Snapdragon (Antirrhinum majus)

Snapdragons are tall plants that produce colorful blooms resembling dragon faces. They come in a variety of colors including pink, red, white, yellow, and purple.

Care Tips:

  • Needs full sun and well-drained soil.
  • Water when the soil feels dry.
  • Deadhead regularly to encourage new blooms.

Lobelia (Lobelia erinus)

Lobelias are trailing plants with bright blue, purple, or white flowers. They are perfect for hanging baskets or as ground cover in your garden.

Care Tips:

  • Prefers partial to full sun.
  • Keep the soil moist, but well-drained.
  • Regularly prune to encourage bushier growth.

Calla Lily (Zantedeschia spp.)

Calla lilies are elegant flowers with a unique funnel-shaped bloom that comes in various colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Care Tips:

  • Requires well-drained, rich soil.
  • Needs full sun to partial shade.
  • Water consistently and keep the soil moist but not soggy.

Kitchen Plants

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)

Tomatoes are vibrant, juicy fruits commonly grown in gardens for salads, sauces, and cooking, offering both flavor and nutrition.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in full sun with rich, well-drained soil.
  • Stake or cage plants to support heavy fruit.
  • Water deeply and consistently to avoid splitting.

Bell Pepper (Capsicum annuum)

Bell peppers are colorful, sweet fruits that add crunch and flavor to dishes, available in green, red, yellow, and orange varieties.

Care Tips:

  • Require warm, well-drained soil and full sunlight.
  • Water evenly to maintain soil moisture.
  • Support plants if fruits become heavy.

Peach (Prunus persica)

Peach trees produce sweet, juicy fruits that are enjoyed fresh or in desserts.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in full sun with well-drained soil.
  • Prune annually to maintain shape and health.
  • Protect from frost during blooming.

Strawberry (Fragaria × ananassa)

Strawberries are sweet, fragrant berries ideal for gardens and containers, producing bright red fruits throughout the growing season.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in full sun with slightly acidic, well-drained soil.
  • Mulch to retain moisture and prevent weeds.
  • Water regularly and remove runners to encourage fruiting.

Cucumber (Cucumis sativus)

Cucumbers are fast-growing vines producing crisp, refreshing fruits, perfect for summer salads and pickling.

Care Tips:

  • Grow in full sun with fertile, loose soil.
  • Train vines onto a trellis for better air circulation.
  • Keep soil consistently moist to prevent bitterness.

Lemon (Citrus limon - Dwarf Varieties)

Dwarf lemon trees produce fragrant blossoms and tangy fruits, making them perfect for small gardens or container growing.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in full sun with well-draining, slightly acidic soil.
  • Fertilize monthly during growing season.
  • Water deeply but allow soil to dry slightly between waterings.

Carrot (Daucus carota)

Carrots are root vegetables known for their sweet flavor and crunchy texture.

Care Tips:

  • Requires loose, sandy soil for root development.
  • Needs full sun and consistent moisture.
  • Thin seedlings to avoid overcrowding.

Radish (Raphanus sativus)

Radishes are fast-growing, peppery root vegetables ideal for quick harvests in cool seasons.

Care Tips:

  • Sow seeds directly into well-prepared soil in full sun.
  • Keep soil evenly moist for tender roots.
  • Harvest promptly to avoid woody texture.

Peas (Pisum sativum)

Peas are cool-season crops producing sweet, tender pods perfect for fresh eating or cooking.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in early spring in fertile, well-drained soil.
  • Provide trellises for climbing varieties.
  • Water consistently to keep plants productive.

Spinach (Spinacia oleracea)

Spinach is a nutritious leafy green that grows quickly in cool weather, ideal for salads and cooking.

Care Tips:

  • Plant in early spring or fall in fertile, moist soil.
  • Water regularly to prevent bolting.
  • Harvest outer leaves first to prolong the season.
